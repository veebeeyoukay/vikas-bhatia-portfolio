### Smart Garage FAQs

**Q: What is a smart garage door and is it worth it?**

A: A smart garage door system adds internet connectivity, remote control, and automation to your garage door. Worth it for South Tampa homeowners because:

**Features:**
- Open/close from anywhere via smartphone
- Status notifications (did I close the garage?)
- Scheduled automatic closing
- Activity history
- Guest access sharing
- Package delivery enablement

**Investment:** $150-$800 depending on whether upgrading existing opener or installing new

**Worth it if you:**
- Have forgotten to close your garage door
- Want secure in-garage package delivery
- Travel and want to check garage status
- Have household members arriving at various times
- Want garage integrated with smart home

**Q: Can I add smart features to my existing garage door opener?**

A: Yes, most garage door openers from the past 20 years can be upgraded:

**MyQ Smart Garage Hub:** Adds connectivity to most major brands ($30-$50)
**Brand-specific upgrades:** Chamberlain/LiftMaster, Genie, Overhead Door offer brand-compatible smart modules
**Universal solutions:** Meross, Nexx, and others work with most openers

**Installation:** 15-30 minutes for basic setup; professional configuration recommended for automation integration

**Q: How does in-garage delivery work?**

A: In-garage delivery (Amazon Key, Walmart) allows drivers to place packages inside your closed garage:

**How it works:**
1. You enable in-garage delivery in Amazon/Walmart app
2. Delivery driver arrives and requests access via their app
3. Smart garage system verifies delivery and opens door
4. Driver places package inside
5. Door closes automatically
6. You receive video confirmation

**Requirements:**
- MyQ-compatible smart garage opener
- Amazon Prime or Walmart+ membership
- Camera (optional but recommended)

**Security:** Driver access is one-time, limited duration, and video-recorded. Many South Tampa homeowners prefer this to porch packages.

### Home Inventory FAQs

**Q: Do I really need a home inventory?**

A: For South Tampa homeowners with significant possessions, yes:

**Industry data:**
- Average homeowner underestimates possessions by 40-60%
- Post-loss recall typically misses 30% of items
- Professional inventory increases claim recovery 25-35%

**When inventory is essential:**
- Homes with $500k+ in contents
- Collections (art, wine, jewelry)
- Frequent travelers with valuable items
- Hurricane-zone residents (Florida)
- Homes with significant electronics
- Families with estate planning needs

**Investment:** $1,000-$3,000 for professional inventory
**ROI:** First insurance claim typically recoups entire investment

**Q: How do I document high-value items for insurance?**

A: High-value items require specific documentation:

**For each item:**
1. Multiple high-resolution photographs (overall, details, markings)
2. Proof of purchase (receipt, invoice, auction record)
3. Current appraisal from qualified appraiser
4. Authentication certificates if applicable
5. Provenance documentation for art/antiques
6. Serial numbers for watches, electronics

**Storage:**
- Cloud backup with secure encryption
- Physical copies in safe deposit box
- Shared access with insurance agent/attorney

**Review schedule:** Appraisals should be updated every 2-3 years for value accuracy

**Q: What should I use for home inventory software?**

A: Software choice depends on your needs:

**For general home inventory:**
- Sortly: Most comprehensive, excellent interface, $49-$149/year
- Encircle: Insurance-focused, great claim features, ~$100/year
- Nest Egg: Visual organization, free basic version

**For specific collections:**
- CellarTracker: Wine (free with premium option)
- Artwork Archive: Art collections ($8-$33/month)
- Watchbox: Timepieces (varies)

**For simple documentation:**
- Liberty Mutual Home Gallery: Free, insurance-focused
- Spreadsheet with cloud photos: Free, requires discipline

Most South Tampa homeowners benefit from dedicated software ($50-$150/year) versus free solutions.
