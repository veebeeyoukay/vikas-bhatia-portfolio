## Visual Identity Guidelines

### Color Palette

**Primary Color:** Orange (#ea580c)
- Represents energy, transformation, enthusiasm
- Use for headers, buttons, primary CTAs

**Secondary Color:** Deep Orange (#c2410c)
- Use for accents, hover states, secondary elements

**Accent Color:** Slate Blue (#475569)
- Use for balance, professionalism
- Represents order, structure

**Supporting Colors:**
- White (#ffffff) - Clean space, transformation result
- Light Gray (#f3f4f6) - Section backgrounds
- Dark Gray (#1f2937) - Body text
- Success Green (#10b981) - Completed, organized
- Warm Beige (#fef3c7) - Friendly warmth

### Typography

**Headlines:** Bold, impactful sans-serif
- Recommended: Montserrat, Outfit, or similar
- Weight: 700-800
- Size: 36-48px for main headlines

**Body Text:** Clean, readable sans-serif
- Recommended: Inter, Source Sans
- Weight: 400-500
- Size: 16-18px
- Line height: 1.5-1.6

**Accent Text:** Strong medium weight
- Use for callouts, before/after labels
- Weight: 600

### Logo Concepts

**Direction 1: Garage Door + Transformation**
- Stylized garage door opening to reveal order
- Before/after implied in design
- Strong, memorable shape

**Direction 2: Rescue Theme**
- Life preserver or rescue element
- Playful nod to "rescue" in name
- Friendly, approachable

**Direction 3: Organization Grid**
- Geometric shapes representing order
- Abstract shelving or storage units
- Clean, modern feel

### Imagery Style

**Photography:**
- Dramatic before/after transformations
- Clean, organized garage spaces
- Storage solutions in action
- Happy homeowners in reclaimed spaces
- Junk removal in progress
- Tampa home exteriors

**Key Image Types:**
- Before: Cluttered, overwhelming garages

- Process: Team working, hauling, organizing

**Illustrations (if used):**
- Storage/organization icons

## Design Direction Notes

### Do's
- Emphasize transformation (before → after)
- Use energetic, warm colors
- Show dramatic results
- Include people in organized spaces
- Make it feel achievable, not intimidating

### Don'ts
- Don't shame the "before" condition
- Don't look clinical or corporate
- Don't forget the emotional satisfaction
- Don't use only empty organized spaces (show function)
- Don't make organization look sterile

### Inspiration References
- Home renovation show marketing (Property Brothers, etc.)
- Marie Kondo / The Home Edit branding
- Satisfying cleaning content aesthetic
- Before/after transformation accounts

The final designs should:
- Feel energetic and transformative
